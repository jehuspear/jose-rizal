The Philippines a Century Hence

A profound essay written by Jose Rizal in 1889-1890, published in La Solidaridad, where he analyzed the possible future of the Philippines in the next hundred years and the various factors that could influence its development or decay.

About the Essay

"The Philippines a Century Hence" ("Filipinas Dentro de Cien Años") is a socio-political essay written by Dr. José Rizal, first published serially in the Barcelona-based periodical La Solidaridad from September 1889 to January 1890.

In this seminal work, Rizal examines the historical causes of the Philippines' decline during Spanish colonization and forecasts the archipelago's future over the next century.

He discusses the potential for reform under Spanish rule, the possibility of violent revolution, and the threat of foreign intervention.

Rizal emphasizes the importance of education, self-determination, and the recognition of Filipino rights as pivotal factors that would shape the nation's destiny.

Jose Rizal: A Brief Biography

José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda (June 19, 1861 – December 30, 1896) was a Filipino nationalist, writer, and polymath during the tail end of the Spanish colonial period in the Philippines. Born in Calamba, Laguna, Rizal was the seventh of eleven children in a well-to-do family. He displayed exceptional intellectual abilities from a young age, mastering multiple languages and excelling in various fields of study.

Rizal during his university years

Rizal pursued higher education in Manila, obtaining a Bachelor of Arts degree from the Ateneo Municipal de Manila. He later studied medicine at the University of Santo Tomas but completed his medical degree at the Universidad Central de Madrid in Spain, specializing in ophthalmology. His desire to cure his mother's eye ailment motivated this specialization.

While in Europe, Rizal became actively involved in the Propaganda Movement, advocating for political reforms in the Philippines through peaceful means. He authored several significant works, including the novels "Noli Me Tángere" and "El Filibusterismo", which exposed the injustices of Spanish colonial rule and inspired a sense of nationalism among Filipinos.

The historic moment of Rizal's martyrdom

Rizal's reformist ideas and writings led to his arrest upon returning to the Philippines. He was exiled to Dapitan in Mindanao for four years. In 1896, amidst the outbreak of the Philippine Revolution, he was arrested, tried for sedition, and executed by firing squad on December 30, 1896, in Manila. His martyrdom awakens the Filipinos to pursue independence, and he is revered as a national hero of the Philippines.
