The Philippines a Century Hence

Filipinas Dentro De Cien Años

First published serially in La Solidaridad, Barcelona (September 1889 - January 1890) by Jose Rizal

Historical Context

"Filipinas Dentro de Cien Años" or "The Philippines a Century Hence" was written during a crucial period in Philippine history, between 1889 and 1890. This era was marked by growing Filipino nationalism and increasing resistance to Spanish colonial rule. The essay was published in La Solidaridad, a newspaper that served as the voice of the Philippine reform movement in Spain.

During this time, the Philippines had been under Spanish colonial rule for over three centuries (300 years). The Spanish colonial system was characterized by:

  • Strict social hierarchy based on race and birthplace
  • Limited access to education for native Filipinos
  • Economic restrictions and monopolies
  • Religious dominance of the Catholic Church

Spolarium by Juan Luna which portrays the Spanish colonial period in the Philippines

The rise of Filipino nationalism

The essay was written in the context of the Propaganda Movement, a literary and cultural organization formed by Filipino emigrants in Europe. These intellectuals, known as Ilustrados, advocated for:

  • Equal rights for Filipinos
  • Representation in the Spanish Parliament
  • Freedom of expression and assembly
  • Secularization of parishes
"The essay reflects the growing consciousness among educated Filipinos about their national identity and their desire for reforms in the colonial system."

Plot Summary

Part I: The Past

In this opening section, Rizal examines the historical factors that led to the Philippines' current state under Spanish colonization. He discusses:

  • The pre-colonial civilization of the Philippines
  • The impact of Spanish colonization on Filipino society
  • The gradual awakening of Filipino consciousness
  • The role of education and exposure to European liberal ideas

Spanish military presence in the Philippines

Part II: The Present

Growing Filipino nationalism and resistance

Rizal analyzes the current situation of the Philippines, focusing on:

  • The growing discontent among Filipinos
  • The resistance to Spanish abuses
  • The rise of education and political consciousness
  • The demands for reform and equal rights

Part III: The Future

Rizal presents three possible scenarios for the Philippines' future:

  1. Spanish Reforms: The Philippines remains under Spain but with significant reforms and equal rights for Filipinos.
  2. Independence: The Philippines gains independence through revolution or peaceful means.
  3. Colonization by Another Power: Another colonial power takes control of the Philippines if Spain loses its grip.

Part IV: Conclusion

In the final part, Rizal:

  • Emphasizes the inevitability of Philippine independence
  • Calls for peaceful reforms and education as the path to progress
  • Warns against violent revolution unless peaceful means fail
  • Expresses hope for the Philippines' future as a free nation
"The Philippines will defend with inexpressible valor the liberty secured at the price of so much blood and sacrifice."
